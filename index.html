SA1 Introduction

Statistical Areas Level 1 (SA1) are geographical areas built from whole Mesh Blocks and it have generally been designed as the smallest unit for the release of census data.SA1 is designed to identify Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities with an aim to exclude as much of the non-indigenous population as possible.SA1s generally have a population of 200 to 800 persons, and an average population of about 400 persons and are designed to be either a predominantly rural or predominantly urban in character.In the area demarcation,SA1 is designed as a feature of the city and the countryside, with different grades based on population density. In terms of whether the population density per square kilometer exceeds 200, the areas with a population density of more than 200 people per square kilometer are designed to have more large urban facilities such as prisons and defense bases than areas with a population density of less than 200 people per square kilometer. However,there is a zero SA1 situation. Zero SA1 is zero population or nominal population of SA1. They were created to represent large uninhabited areas that are difficult to combine with the densely populated SA1 surrounding them.SA1 is usually connected internally by road transport and has non-spatial SA1 for mobility, offshore, shipping, and no usual address in each state and region. Besides,SA1 is identified by either an 11-bit fully layered code identifier or a truncated 7-bit code identifier consisting of state and territory, SA2 and SA1 identifiers. Since SA1 was established in August 2016 until now, it has been predicted that new code may need to be allocated in the future.

Background

The difference between people with a higher education and those with lower education is not material, but pattern and horizon.The undergraduate students not only receive the knowledge of the university for four years, but also receive the cultivation of the cultural atmosphere in the university for several years.The more they learn, and the higher degree they reach, the more they will be able to analyse the situation fully and rationally from a higher position. The more they are able to make rational choices in their petty and long-term interests. When they step into the society, they will not stop for the small profit in front of them. Their deep thinking supports them to skip the underlying work of making a living, but directly to the creative and constructive field, thus becoming the top few high-income people in this pyramid society.Most people who earn a lot of money have an obsession. Because they want to achieve their goals, they will constantly push themselves and learn. In addition, self-discipline and good interpersonal skills are also standard among high-income people. Highly educated and highly paid people have three things in common: the right market signals, abundant human resources, and strong alumni relationships (social capital). The only thing that highly educated people need to become high-income people is time, and if they have enough time to master these three points, they will enter the ranks of high earners.Higher education and higher income are positively correlated. And with the continuous improvement of educational level, income will also achieve synchronous level crossing.
